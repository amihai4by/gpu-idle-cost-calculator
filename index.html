Why idle GPUs are the biggest line item nobody watches

A GPU costs the same whether it's running at 100% or sitting at 0% — you pay for the allocation, not the work. So utilization is a direct multiplier on your real cost. Run a fleet at 30% average utilization and you're paying 3.3× per useful GPU-hour versus a fleet at 100%. Across a rack of H100s that's tens of thousands of dollars a month evaporating on cards that are allocated, powered, billed — and idle.

How teams actually cut it

  • Measure per-GPU utilization — DCGM → Prometheus → Grafana, per card, per pod. You can't cut what you can't see.
  • Share GPUs for dev/notebooks/light inference (time-slicing) or partition them (MIG) so small models stop eating whole cards.
  • Cap hoarding with per-namespace GPU quotas.
  • Run interruptible work on spot nodes (60–90% cheaper).
  • Reclaim idle allocated GPUs automatically instead of hoping someone notices.

FAQ

How much do idle GPUs cost?
An idle H100 (~$2.50/hr) burns ~$1,800/month at 0% utilization. At 30% fleet utilization, ~70% of your GPU bill is paying for idle time.

What's a good GPU utilization target?
Most fleets sit at 20–40%. Getting to 60–70% typically cuts the bill 30–60% for the same work.

How do I reduce Kubernetes GPU costs?
Measure utilization, share/partition GPUs, quota them, use spot for interruptible jobs, and auto-reclaim idle allocations.
